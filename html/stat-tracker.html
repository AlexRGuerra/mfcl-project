Discipline Tracker

In soccer, yellow and red cards are crucial disciplinary tools employed by referees to regulate player behavior on the field. A yellow card serves as a caution for less severe infractions or persistent misconduct, with common offenses including reckless tackles, dissent, and unsporting conduct. Accumulating multiple yellow cards can lead to additional consequences, such as suspensions.

A red card is a more severe penalty, resulting in the immediate expulsion of the player from the game. It is issued for serious offenses such as violent conduct, or accumulating two yellow cards in a single match. When a player receives a red card, their team must play with a numerical disadvantage, posing a challenge for their team.
